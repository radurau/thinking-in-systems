Building Block № 1

Stocks & Flows

A stock is what accumulates. A flow is what changes it. Meadows calls this chapter "Bathtubs 101."

Bank account — salary in, spending out Trust — built in drops, lost in buckets Team workload — requests in, delivery out

A stock changes only through its flows — and you can fill a bathtub by closing the drain.

Archetypes

The eight system traps.

Meadows catalogued them. Recurring structures, reliably bad outcomes — no villains required.

Eight recurring structures. No villains required.

press → to meet the first one

Policy resistance

Fixes that fail. Everyone pushes, the system pushes back — the quick fix relieves the symptom while quietly feeding the problem.

Tragedy of the commons

Everyone acts sensibly — the shared thing dies anyway. The meeting-room calendar. The oceans. The on-call rota.

Drift to low performance

Bad becomes normal one small excuse at a time. Nobody decided to have flaky tests — the standard just… drifted.

Escalation

"I'll raise you one." Price wars, feature wars, the reply-all thread. It's a reinforcing loop — it compounds.

Success to the successful

Winners get the resources to keep winning. The strong team gets the best projects — which makes it stronger.

Shifting the burden

The workaround works, so the real fix never gets built. Meadows' word for this one is addiction. Every "temporary" process is five years old.

Rule beating

Obeying the letter of the rule while defeating its purpose. Hitting the metric, missing the point.

Seeking the wrong goal

You get what you measure — exactly that, and nothing more. The big target was the point; the small one was just easier to hit.

A company

A family

A product team

Your health

The economy

In Practice

Six moves. Any system.

1

Map the system

Draw what connects to what. Paper is fine.

2

Identify stocks and flows

What accumulates? What fills it, what drains it?

3

Look for feedback loops

What amplifies? What pushes back?

4

Notice the delays

When will you actually see the results of acting?

5

Find the leverage points

Where does a small push travel furthest?

6

Change structure, not symptoms

The only change that lasts.

Clock Speed

Same structure. Ten times the spin.

AI doesn't change what your loops do — it changes how fast they do it. Both of them.

Fast loops with human delays overshoot harder.

The Obvious Fix

Machine taps need machine drains.

So we build a second outflow: agents in harnesses — nondeterministic engines inside deterministic constraints.

evals — gate every merge guardrails — bound the blast radius rollbacks — cheap and rehearsed

A nondeterministic engine is fine — inside a deterministic harness.

The Familiarity Trap

Same color. Same plate. Not the same car.

You start the engine every morning without fear — because you believe it's the same car as yesterday.

Perception says "same car." The system under the hood already changed.
